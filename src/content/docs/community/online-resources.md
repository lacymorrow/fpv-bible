FPV knowledge is scattered across the internet. This page collects the most valuable resources in one place so you don't have to stumble around finding them.

## Reference Sites

### Oscar Liang — oscarliang.com
The single most comprehensive FPV reference site. Oscar maintains 200+ in-depth articles covering every aspect of building and flying FPV drones. If you have a technical question about FPV, Oscar has probably written about it.

**Best for:** Component deep-dives, Betaflight tutorials, build guides, FPV system comparisons, product reviews.

### FPV Know-It-All — fpvknowitall.com
Joshua Bardwell's curated shopping list. The "what should I buy?" resource. Every component category has hand-picked recommendations with explanations of why each product made the list.

**Best for:** Parts selection, shopping lists, beginner build kits.

### GetFPV Learn — getfpv.com/learn
GetFPV's educational section. Structured learning paths from beginner to advanced, covering components, building, flying, and tuning. Backed by a major FPV retailer.

**Best for:** Structured beginner learning, build logs, flight academy.

### Betaflight Wiki — betaflight.com/docs/wiki
The official Betaflight documentation. Essential for understanding firmware settings, CLI commands, and feature configuration.

**Best for:** Betaflight-specific configuration, firmware features, CLI reference.

## YouTube Channels

### Joshua Bardwell
The "FPV Know-It-All." Covers everything from beginner tutorials to advanced debugging. Famous for his "how to fix" videos where he diagnoses viewer problems. The most comprehensive FPV YouTube channel.

### Oscar Liang
Written articles brought to video. Detailed, technical, well-researched content. Less frequent uploads but every video is valuable.

### Rotor Riot
Team-based channel with high production value. Great freestyle and racing content, beginner guides, and product reviews. Chad Nowak, Le Drib, and other well-known pilots.

### Mr Steele
One of the most recognized freestyle pilots. Known for his smooth style and "Steele" freestyle approach. Good for inspiration and understanding what great freestyle looks like.

### UAVfutures
Australian FPV channel famous for budget builds. The "99$ build" series made FPV accessible to people on tight budgets. Practical, no-nonsense content.

### Nick Burns (Nurk FPV)
Professional FPV pilot who films for TV and commercials. Content focuses on cinematic FPV, professional drone work, and real-world applications.

### Chris Rosser
Deep technical content. PID tuning, filter theory, Betaflight internals. For pilots who want to understand the "why" behind every setting.

### Gal Kremer
Digital FPV system comparisons and reviews. One of the best resources for understanding DJI, Walksnail, and HDZero differences.

## Forums and Communities

### Reddit
- **r/fpv** (~350k members): The main FPV subreddit. Good for questions, build checks, and community discussion. Check the FAQ and Wiki before posting.
- **r/Multicopter**: Older, broader multirotor community. Good archives.
- **r/multicopterbuilds**: Focused on build help and build sharing.
- **r/TinyWhoop**: Specifically for whoop pilots.

### Discord
- **FPV Freedom Coalition**: Active community, helpful for beginners
- **ExpressLRS**: Official ELRS Discord for link-specific help
- **Betaflight**: Official Betaflight Discord
- **Frame/brand specific**: Most major brands (iFlight, GEPRC, BetaFPV) have Discord servers

### Facebook Groups
- **FPV Drone Pilots** (huge, general)
- **Tiny Whoop** (whoop-specific)
- **FPV Buy/Sell/Trade** (used gear marketplace)
- Regional/local FPV groups (search your city + "FPV")

### IntoFPV — intofpv.com
A traditional forum for FPV discussion. Less active than Reddit/Discord but has deep archived threads and a knowledgeable community.
